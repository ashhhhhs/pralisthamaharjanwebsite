Artist statement

Creating characters, symbols, and colors that feel personal long after the first look.

Pralistha Maharjan works across acrylic painting, graphite studies, character concepts, and mixed-media experiments. Every piece begins with a feeling, then becomes a face, a ritual mark, or a small world with its own atmosphere.

Here, art is a personal record of color, culture, and imagination.

One person / one practice

A single artist building a language of faces.

The work blends mythic figures, intimate portraits, pop-inspired color, and quiet studies from the studio. It is made for people who want art with presence, story, and a human hand behind every mark.

Voice of the artist

Different materials overlap until one personal image appears.

A sketch can become a painting. A mask can become a character. A portrait can become a memory. This site follows the way one person turns fragments into finished visual worlds.

Studio rhythm

A layered practice, from first mark to final atmosphere.

01

Collect

Symbols, reference faces, clothing details, cultural memory, and tiny visual fragments enter the sketchbook first.

02

Shape

Graphite studies find the structure, then color tests decide whether the image should feel devotional, strange, warm, or electric.

03

Resolve

The final artwork is edited until the face, color, and negative space feel like they belong to the same memory.
